                  📝 INPUT FORMS & DATA ENTRY

                  🏢 Company Setup ▼

                  This is the foundation of your business plan. Information entered here flows through to the PDF cover page, financial projections, BAS statements, and valuation calculations.

                  Company Logo — Upload your business logo (PNG or JPG, max 2MB). This appears on the PDF report cover page. Use a clean, high-resolution version for best results.
                  Business Name & Industry — Your registered business name and industry sector. The industry selection helps when loading pitch deck templates and determines appropriate valuation multipliers.
                  Business Address — Full registered address. This is displayed on the PDF cover page and gives your business plan a professional, legitimate appearance for investors.
                  Currency — Select your operating currency. This determines the currency symbol shown throughout the app and in reports.
                  Tax Rate (GST/VAT %) — Your applicable tax rate. Used in BAS Statement calculations. Default is 10% (Australian GST). Set to your local rate (e.g., 20% for UK VAT, 0% if not applicable).
                  Revenue Growth Rate (%) — The annual percentage you expect revenue to grow. Default is 15%. This is a critical input — it drives all 5-year projections, dashboard charts, valuation DCF calculations, and scenario comparisons. Be realistic: 3-8% for mature businesses, 10-25% for growing SMBs, 30%+ for startups.
                  Expense Growth Rate (%) — The annual percentage you expect expenses to increase. Default is 8%. Should be lower than revenue growth for margins to improve over time. Typical range: 5-10%.

                  💼 Startup Costs ▼

                  One-time costs required to launch your business. These feed into the Balance Sheet as assets and are part of the total capital investment used in ROI calculations.

                  Cost Name — Describe the expense clearly (e.g., "Website Development", "Office Fitout", "Legal & Registration Fees", "Initial Inventory").
                  Amount ($) — The one-time cost in dollars. Enter the full amount, not monthly payments.
                  Category — Helps organise costs. Common categories: Equipment, Technology, Legal, Marketing, Inventory, Renovations, Licenses.

                  💡 Tip: Include everything you need to spend before opening day. Investors want to see you've thought through all upfront costs. Don't forget legal fees, accounting setup, branding, and first/last month's rent.

                  💰 Revenue Sources ▼

                  Add each way your business earns money. Multiple revenue streams make your business more resilient and attractive to investors. Revenue data drives the dashboard, projections, valuations, and PDF report.

                  Source Name — A clear label for this revenue stream (e.g., "Coffee Sales", "Monthly Subscriptions", "Consulting Retainers").
                  Type — Choose how this revenue works:
                  • Product Sales (Qty-based) — Physical or digital products. Opens the calculator where you enter quantity, selling price, and cost price per unit.
                  • Service / Subscription / Consulting / Other (Fixed) — Enter a fixed monthly amount and COGS directly.
                  Product Sales Calculator Fields:
                  • Quantity per Period — How many units you sell per week/month/year
                  • Period — Select week, month, or year. Weekly quantities are auto-converted to monthly (×4.33)
                  • Selling Price per Unit — What the customer pays per item
                  • Cost Price per Unit — What it costs you to produce/acquire each item (COGS)
                  The calculator automatically shows monthly quantity, revenue, COGS, and gross profit.
                  Monthly Amount (Fixed types) — Your expected monthly revenue from this source.
                  COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) — The direct costs to deliver this revenue. For services, this might be subcontractor costs or materials. For SaaS, it's hosting and infrastructure. Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS.

                  💸 Expenses ▼

                  Recurring monthly business expenses (not COGS — those go in Revenue). These are your operating overheads that exist regardless of how much you sell.

                  Expense Name — Clear description (e.g., "Office Rent", "Software Subscriptions", "Phone & Internet").
                  Category — Helps organise in reports and pie charts:
                  • Fixed Cost — Doesn't change with sales (rent, insurance)
                  • Variable Cost — Changes with sales volume (packaging, shipping)
                  • Marketing — Ads, SEO, social media
                  • Utilities / Insurance / Other — Self-explanatory
                  Monthly Amount ($) — The recurring monthly cost. Annual costs should be divided by 12.

                  👥 Payroll / Employees ▼

                  Track your team and labour costs. Payroll is separate from expenses because it's calculated differently — salaries are annual, and the highest salary is used for SDE valuation calculations.

                  Employee Name & Role — Full name and job title. Helps with team section of your business plan.
                  Employment Type — Full-Time, Part-Time, or Contractor. Important for legal and tax purposes.
                  Annual Salary ($) — Total annual salary before tax. This is converted to monthly for all calculations. Include super/pension contributions if applicable.
                  Benefits/Allowances (Monthly $) — Additional monthly costs like health insurance, car allowance, or phone allowance.

                  🚗 Motor Vehicles ▼

                  Track business vehicles as assets. The purchase price appears on the Balance Sheet, while monthly costs (payment, insurance, fuel, maintenance) are included in the Cash Flow Statement.

                  Vehicle Description — Make, model, and year (e.g., "2024 Toyota HiLux - Delivery Van").
                  Purchase Price ($) — Full purchase price. This is the asset value shown on your Balance Sheet.
                  Monthly Costs — Separate fields for loan payment, insurance, fuel, and maintenance. Enter your expected monthly averages.

                  🏦 Business Loans ▼

                  Track all business debt. Loan amounts appear on the Balance Sheet as liabilities and factor into the Debt-to-Equity ratio. They're also included in total capital invested for ROI calculations.

                  Loan Description — Name of the loan or lender (e.g., "ANZ Business Loan", "Equipment Finance").
                  Principal Amount ($) — The total amount borrowed, not the monthly payment.
                  Interest Rate (%) — Annual interest rate. Used for financial projections and cost of capital.
                  Term (Years) — Loan duration. Shorter terms mean higher monthly payments but less total interest paid.

                  👔 Directors ▼

                  Track company directors, their equity stakes, and compensation. Director equity is deducted from Founder Equity in the ROI analytics page.

                  Director Name & Role — Full name and title (CEO, CTO, CFO, Non-Executive Director, etc.).
                  Equity Ownership (%) — The percentage of the company this director owns. Total equity across directors + investors should not exceed 100%.
                  Annual Compensation ($) — Total yearly pay including base salary and director fees. This is separate from the Payroll page.

                  💼 Investors ▼

                  Track funding rounds and investor contributions. Investor funding is the primary input for ROI, Payback Period, and NPV calculations — it's the capital that expects a return.

                  Investor Name — Individual or firm name (e.g., "Sarah Chen - Angel", "Sequoia Capital").
                  Investment Type — The round or type: Angel, Venture Capital, Seed, Series A, Series B, or Other.
                  Investment Amount ($) — The cash contributed. This directly feeds into Total Capital Invested, which is used to calculate ROI (profit ÷ investment), Payback Period (investment ÷ monthly profit), and NPV (discounted future returns minus investment).
                  Equity Given (%) — The ownership percentage given in exchange for the investment. Combined with director equity, this determines your Founder Equity (what you retain). Example: $250K investment for 15% equity implies a $1.67M pre-money valuation.

                  📝 Pitch Deck Builder ▼

                  Build your business narrative. Select an industry template as a starting guide, then customise each section. All content flows into the "Business Plan Narrative" section of your PDF export.

                  Industry Template — Select your industry to load pre-written demo content for all 11 sections. Templates are available for SaaS, E-commerce, Consulting, Manufacturing, Food & Beverage, Healthcare, Real Estate, and FinTech. Edit everything to match your business.
                  The 11 Sections:
                  • Executive Summary — 2-3 sentence overview of your entire business
                  • Mission Statement — Why your company exists
                  • Vision Statement — Where you're heading in 5-10 years
                  • Problem Statement — The pain point you solve
                  • Solution — How your product/service solves it
                  • Target Market — Who your customers are and market size
                  • Business Model — How you make money
                  • Competitive Advantage — Why you win against competitors
                  • Financial Projections — Revenue targets and growth narrative
                  • Team — Key people and their credentials
                  • The Ask — How much you're raising and how you'll use it

                  💡 Tip: Empty sections are automatically skipped in the PDF export. Write in plain language — avoid jargon. Investors read hundreds of plans; clarity wins.

                  🎯 Goals ▼

                  Set business milestones and track progress. Goals help you stay focused and demonstrate traction to investors.

                  💡 Tip: Use SMART goals — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Instead of "Grow revenue", try "Reach $50K monthly revenue by December 2026".

                  📊 REPORTS, ANALYTICS & OUTPUTS

                  📊 Dashboard ▼

                  Your financial overview at a glance. All numbers are calculated automatically from your revenue, expense, and payroll data.

                  Summary Cards — Total Revenue, Total Expenses (including COGS), Net Profit, and Profit Margin. All monthly figures.
                  5 Charts — Revenue vs Expenses (5-year line chart), Revenue Mix (doughnut), Monthly Cash Flow (bar chart), Expense Breakdown (pie chart), and Profit Margin Trend (line chart). All respond to your growth rate settings.

                  💡 Tip: If charts aren't showing, try navigating to another page and back. Charts need data — add at least one revenue source and one expense to see them populate.

                  📑 Financial Statements ▼

                  Income Statement (Profit & Loss) — Revenue minus COGS = Gross Profit, minus operating expenses and payroll = Net Income. Shows whether operations are profitable. This is the first thing investors look at.
                  Cash Flow Statement — Operating activities (profit from operations), Investing activities (money spent on assets like vehicles and equipment), Financing activities (money from loans). Net Cash Flow shows actual cash position.
                  Balance Sheet — Assets (cash, receivables, equipment) = Liabilities (payables, loans) + Equity. A snapshot of what the business owns vs owes at any point in time.
                  KPIs — Gross Margin %, Net Margin %, ROI %, and Break-Even Revenue. Quick health indicators derived from the statements above.

                  🧾 BAS Statement ▼

                  Business Activity Statement for Australian GST reporting. Shows GST collected on sales minus GST paid on purchases. The difference is what you owe to or are owed by the ATO.

                  The GST rate is pulled from your Company Setup tax rate setting. Select the quarter (Q1-Q4) to see quarterly figures. If you're not in Australia or don't charge GST, this page can be ignored.

                  💎 Business Valuation ▼

                  Revenue Multiple

                  Formula: Annual Revenue × Industry Multiplier
                  Best for high-growth companies. Typical multipliers: Traditional 1-2x, Services 2-3x, Tech 4-6x, High-growth SaaS 8-15x.

                  SDE Multiple (Seller's Discretionary Earnings)

                  Formula: (Net Profit + Owner's Salary) × Multiplier (capped at 4x)
                  The most common SMB acquisition method. SDE represents what an owner-operator actually earns. The owner's salary is estimated as the highest salary on your payroll.

                  DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)

                  Formula: Sum of future cash flows discounted to today + terminal value
                  Projects 5 years of cash flows using your growth rates, then discounts them at the Discount Rate (default 12%) to reflect risk and time value of money. Terminal Value represents perpetual value beyond year 5.

                  Sensitivity Analysis — The table shows how your valuation changes across different growth rates and multipliers. Your current position is highlighted in blue. Useful for showing investors a range rather than a single number.

                  ⚠️ These are financial estimates only. Real valuations also consider brand value, IP, customer contracts, market conditions, and qualitative factors. Consult a professional valuator for actual transactions.

                  📐 ROI & Business Analytics ▼

                  ROI (%) — (Annual Net Profit ÷ Total Capital Invested) × 100. Capital includes investor funding + startup costs + loans. A 100% ROI means you doubled the investment in one year.
                  Payback Period — Total Capital Invested ÷ Monthly Net Profit. How long until investors recover their money. Under 24 months is attractive; under 12 is excellent.
                  Break-Even Revenue — Annual Fixed Costs ÷ Gross Margin Ratio. The revenue needed to cover all overheads. Below this = losses.
                  NPV (Net Present Value) — Today's value of 5 years of future profits minus the initial investment, discounted at 12%. Positive = good investment.
                  Financial Health Score (0-100) — Composite score based on: profit margins (25 pts), gross margins (25 pts), ROI (25 pts), revenue diversification (15 pts), and debt levels (10 pts).
                  Unit Economics — Per-unit profitability for product-based revenue. Shows selling price, cost price, unit profit, unit margin, volume, and monthly gross profit for each product.
                  Scenario Comparison — Three projections: Worst Case (half growth, 50% more expenses), Base Case (your settings), Best Case (50% more growth, 30% less expense growth). Shows Years 1, 3, and 5 revenue and profit.
